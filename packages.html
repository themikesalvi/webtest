FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about our packages and process

How does the corporate retainer work?

We start with a discovery call to understand your brand, goals, and audience. From there we build a content strategy, agree on deliverables, and begin producing on a monthly cycle. Retainers are billed monthly and include a dedicated account contact who manages your relationship with XLNZ from start to finish.

How long does production typically take?

Timelines vary by project type. We'll provide a detailed production schedule during your initial consultation so you know exactly what to expect and when. For retainer clients, content is delivered on an agreed monthly schedule so your brand stays consistent.

Can I customize a package to fit my specific needs?

Absolutely. Our packages are starting points. We can adjust deliverables, volume, and scope to fit your exact goals and budget. Reach out via our contact page and we'll build something that works for you.

What payment options do you accept?

We accept credit cards, bank transfers, Zelle, and PayPal. For podcast packages, we offer a structured payment plan: 25% down to book your dates, first two episodes released when your balance hits 50%, and the full season delivered at 100% paid. Corporate retainers are billed monthly.

Do you provide raw footage or audio files?

All packages include delivery of final edited files. Raw footage or audio can be provided for an additional fee — just let us know upfront so we can factor it into the workflow and storage plan.

Can I upgrade my package after starting?

Yes, any time. If you want to add episodes, expand your retainer tier, or add services mid-engagement, we'll adjust your package and credit any payments already made toward the new total. We want the relationship to grow with your brand.

Do you travel outside Philadelphia?

Yes. We are Philadelphia-based but built to travel. Corporate retainer clients and larger projects can include on-location shoots wherever your brand needs us. Travel costs are discussed during the proposal stage.

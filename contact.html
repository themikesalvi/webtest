FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about working with XLNZ Productions

How far in advance should I book your services?

We recommend booking at least 2 to 3 weeks in advance for smaller projects and 1 to 2 months for larger productions. That said, if your timeline is tighter, reach out anyway. If we have availability we will make it work.

Do you work with clients outside of Philadelphia?

Yes. We are Philadelphia-based and built to travel. We work with clients throughout the tri-state area and beyond. For projects outside our immediate area, travel costs will be clearly outlined in your project quote so there are no surprises.

What is included in your standard packages?

Every package includes professional recording, full editing, digital delivery, and a strategy consultation. Higher-tier packages include additional services like custom intros, sound design, and color grading. You can view the full breakdown on our Packages page.

How long does it take to receive the final product?

Turnaround depends on the scope of your project. Podcast episodes typically take 3 to 5 business days. Stand-up specials and brand video productions may take 2 to 3 weeks. We provide a detailed timeline during your consultation and keep you updated throughout.

What is your payment structure?

We typically require a 50% deposit to secure your booking and begin pre-production, with the remaining balance due upon project completion. For larger projects we can arrange milestone-based payments. We accept credit cards, bank transfers, Zelle, and PayPal.

Can I be involved in the editing process?

Absolutely. We encourage client collaboration throughout. Your package includes a set number of revision rounds and we provide preview links so you can review and give feedback before we finalize anything. The goal is always for the final product to match your vision.

What makes XLNZ different from other production companies?

We think like marketers and shoot like filmmakers. Most production companies show up with a camera. We show up with a strategy. Before we ever press record we study your brand, your audience, and your competition so that every creative decision is made with a purpose behind it. That is what separates content that performs from content that just exists.
